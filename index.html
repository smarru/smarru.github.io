About

Suresh Marru is a researcher, open source leader, entrepreneur, and mentor whose work sits at the intersection of artificial intelligence, computational science, and national-scale cyberinfrastructure. He leads the ARTISAN Center at Georgia Tech — a joint center between the College of Computing and the Institute for Data Engineering and Science — dedicated to accelerating scientific discovery through AI.

His career is defined by three parallel commitments: building open, reusable research infrastructure for the national scientific community; translating academic research into real-world products and companies; and investing deeply in the growth of students and early-career professionals.

Research & ARTISAN Center
ARTISAN aims to accelerate advances in science and engineering by integrating cutting-edge artificial intelligence techniques. We are dedicated to fostering interdisciplinary research, cultivating the next generation of AI experts, and developing innovative solutions that address complex challenges in our world.

Current Projects
Nexus
NSF Category I Advanced Computing Infrastructure
Georgia Tech's next-generation AI supercomputer providing a diverse array of AI software and hardware. A universal interface allows researchers to select and compose processes into workflows, enabling Nexus to support research across a wide range of science and engineering fields with seamless scaling from local to national resources.
nexus.gatech.edu ↗
Cybershuttle
NSF · Open Infrastructure
A user-facing cyberinfrastructure providing seamless access to a range of computing resources for researchers. Built on Apache Airavata, it integrates locally deployed agents with centrally hosted middleware — enabling end-to-end access to local, university, cloud, and NSF national computing resources with a user-centered design.
cybershuttle.org ↗
Custos
NSF · Security Middleware
Open source security middleware for the science gateway community, enabling federated authentication with 3,000+ identity providers via CILogon, secure credential storage via Vault, hierarchical group management, and user-level audit logs through an administrative portal.
VizFold
NSF NAIRR · AI4Science
A research initiative making AlphaFold and protein-folding models interpretable, visual, and explainable. Developing new techniques for exploring protein structure predictions and analyzing intermediate network representations, integrated with Cybershuttle for interactive browser-based exploration.
vizfold.github.io ↗
SciGaP
Platform as a Service
Science Gateways Platform as a Service — a rapid development and stable hosting platform for science gateways. Multi-tenanted, language-independent API supporting user management, workflow execution, experiment archiving, and sharing services integrated into web, mobile, and desktop clients.
scigap.org ↗
Open Source Advocacy

Open source software is not just a development methodology for Suresh — it is a philosophy about how research infrastructure should be built and governed. He believes that publicly-funded science requires openly-available, community-maintained tools, and has spent two decades putting that belief into practice.

As an elected member of the Apache Software Foundation and VP & PMC Chair of Apache Airavata, he leads one of the most widely adopted open source platforms in computational science. He has shepherded multiple projects through the Apache Incubator and serves as Google Summer of Code Organization Administrator for the Apache Foundation, actively growing the next generation of open source contributors.

Entrepreneurship & Technology Translation

Suresh believes one of the most important — and undervalued — roles a researcher can play is building a clear pathway from academic discovery to real-world impact. The journey from a published result to a working product used by people requires a different set of skills than research alone, and he has invested in developing them.

Through co-founding and leading multiple ventures, he has navigated the full lifecycle of product development, from early-stage prototyping through commercialization, while remaining grounded in the academic and open source communities that generate the underlying innovations. He brings these lessons back to students and collaborators, helping them understand both the opportunities and the realities of translational work.

Mentoring & Teaching

Mentoring students and early-career professionals is not an obligation Suresh approaches as an afterthought — it is a core part of how he understands his role. He has directly supervised or co-supervised more than 38 graduate students across PhD and Master's programs, and consistently creates environments where students work on real, deployed systems with national impact rather than isolated academic exercises.

His mentoring philosophy is built around three ideas: that students deserve substantive problems worthy of their effort; that the best learning happens at the boundary of research and practice; and that a mentor's job is to open doors — to ideas, networks, and opportunities — that a student couldn't reach alone.

Real Problems, Real Stakes
Students work on deployed systems used by thousands of researchers — not toy problems. The code they write runs in production. That accountability shapes how they think.
Research Meets Practice
Connecting academic rigor with the realities of building and sustaining software. Students leave with both scholarly depth and practical engineering judgment.
Opening Doors
Leveraging networks in academia, open source, and industry to create pathways students couldn't find alone — including Google Summer of Code, Apache community, and startup ecosystems.
